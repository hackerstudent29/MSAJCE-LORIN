Programme Outcomes (PO) — B.Tech Information Technology, MSAJCE

PO1 — Engineering Knowledge: Apply the knowledge of mathematics, science, engineering fundamentals, and an engineering specialization to the solution of complex engineering problems.

PO2 — Problem Analysis: Identify, formulate, review research literature, and analyze complex engineering problems reaching substantiated conclusions using first principles of mathematics, natural sciences, and engineering sciences.

PO3 — Design/Development Of Solutions: Design solutions for complex engineering problems and design system components or processes that meet the specified needs with appropriate consideration for the public health and safety, and the cultural, societal, and environmental considerations.

PO4 — Conduct Investigations of Complex Problems: Use research-based knowledge and research methods including design of experiments, analysis and interpretation of data, and synthesis of the information to provide valid conclusions.

PO5 — Modern Tool Usage: Create, select, and apply appropriate techniques, resources, and modern engineering and IT tools including prediction and modeling to complex engineering activities with an understanding of the limitations.

PO6 — The Engineer and Society: Apply reasoning informed by the contextual knowledge to assess societal, health, safety, legal and cultural issues and the consequent responsibilities relevant to the professional engineering practice.

PO7 — Environment and Sustainability: Understand the impact of the professional engineering solutions in societal and environmental contexts, and demonstrate the knowledge of, and need for sustainable development.

PO8 — Ethics: Apply ethical principles and commit to professional ethics and responsibilities and norms of the engineering practice.

PO9 — Individual and Team Work: Function effectively as an individual, and as a member or leader in diverse teams, and in multi disciplinary settings.

PO10 — Communication: Communicate effectively on complex engineering activities with the engineering community and with society at large.

PO11 — Project Management and Finance: Demonstrate knowledge and understanding of the engineering and management principles and apply these to one's own work.

PO12 — Life-long Learning: Recognize the need for, and have the preparation and ability to engage in independent and life-long learning in the broadest context of technological change.

IT Employment Forecast in India — 2025 to 2030:

The Information Technology (IT) industry in India is poised for strong growth over the next five years, driven by domestic and global trends.

Key Drivers of IT Growth:

1. **Digital Transformation:** Businesses across all sectors are adopting Cloud computing, AI, Big Data, IoT, and Blockchain. Demand for cloud engineers, data scientists, AI specialists, and DevOps engineers will rise.
2. **Cloud Computing:** Cloud market in India is growing rapidly. AWS, Microsoft Azure, and Google Cloud will create high demand for cloud architects, cloud administrators, and cloud security specialists. Hybrid cloud solutions will generate opportunities in cloud migration, cloud operations, and cloud infrastructure management.
3. **Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning:** AI is transforming healthcare, finance, manufacturing, and retail. By 2030, demand will rise for data scientists, AI researchers, machine learning engineers, and AI-enabled software developers. Skills needed: AI ethics, deep learning, AI model deployment.
4. **Cyber Security:** With increasing cyberattacks and regulations (GDPR, Personal Data Protection Bill), demand for cybersecurity experts will grow. Roles: ethical hackers, incident response professionals, security architects, SOC analysts.
5. **Fintech and Blockchain:** India's cashless economy push requires blockchain developers, smart contract auditors, and DeFi specialists.
6. **5G and Telecommunications:** Rollout of 5G will create demand for 5G network engineers, telecommunications architects, and telecom software developers. IoT will see a boom in smart cities, agriculture, and healthcare.
7. **E-Governance and Smart Cities:** Government initiatives like Digital India, Smart Cities Mission, and Make in India will create roles for IT project managers, data engineers, IoT developers, and cloud solutions architects.
8. **Outsourcing and Off-shoring:** India will continue as a global IT outsourcing hub with demand for application developers, IT consultants, systems integrators, and support specialists.
