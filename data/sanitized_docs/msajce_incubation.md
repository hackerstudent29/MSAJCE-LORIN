Sathak Innovation and Incubation Foundation (SIIF) — Overview & Incubation Cell

**Sathak Innovation and Incubation Foundation (SIIF) — Main Objects:**
1. To promote commerce, science, education, and research by helping, establishing, and incubating startups, individuals, and teams with innovative ideas to make prototypes and convert them into products.
2. To promote a culture of innovation-driven entrepreneurship in various fields, including science, engineering, technology, commerce, agriculture, health care, and arts.
3. To establish and manage infrastructure and labs for prototype testing and development, software facilities, training centers, and promote scientific and technological advancements.

**Incubation Cell — Services Offered:**
- Physical office space: approx. 5000 sq.ft.
- Event halls: 100-seater, 400-seater, and 700-seater halls.
- Funding opportunities: grants, seed funding, and connections to potential investors.
- Mentorship programs: guidance from experienced professionals and industry experts.
- Access to a network of investors, venture capitalists, and angel investors.
- Workshops and training for innovation and entrepreneurial skills.

**2. National Innovation and Startup Policy (NISP) — Vision, Mission & Objectives**
**NISP Vision:**
To cater to the needs of student entrepreneurs with innovative ideas and promote a culture of entrepreneurship within the campus to enhance the education system and contribute to national economic growth.

**NISP Mission:**
To develop an ecosystem with the required infrastructure enabling students and faculty to innovate and prototype potential ideas with support from government and industry.

**Short Term Objectives:**
1. Support student groups in prototyping their ideas.
2. Enhance innovation and creative thinking among students.
3. Provide incubation facility for faculty-driven and student/alumni startups.
4. Organize workshops and promote an entrepreneurial culture.
5. Strengthen institute-industry interaction.

**Long Term Objectives:**
1. Collaborate with institutions for world-class facility transfers.
2. Improve the quality of research and attain commercial patents.
3. Provide a platform for students to develop globally recognized products.
4. Create revenue through consultancy and student startups.
5. Raise awareness of IPR-related activities.

**Institution Innovation Council (IIC) — About, Vision, Mission & Office Bearers**

IIC-MSAJCE promotes innovation through the Ministry of Human Resource Development Innovation Cell.

**IIC Functions:**
1. Identify and reward innovations.
2. Organize workshops with entrepreneurs and create a mentor pool.
3. Network with national entrepreneurship organizations.
4. Highlight innovations through an Institution's Innovation Portal.
