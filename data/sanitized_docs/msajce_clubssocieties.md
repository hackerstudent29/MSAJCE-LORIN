3. Science Club — Introduction, Objectives and Activities

Context: Describes the MSAJCE Science Club — its purpose of inviting scientists, three sections (Physics, Chemistry, General), guiding principles, and planned activities including experiments and workshops.

MSAJCE Science Club

Introduction: The Science Club was started with an aim to invite eminent scientists who would share their experience with students and motivate them to gain scientific knowledge. Activities are designed primarily to introduce students to undertake interesting and exciting science and technology projects. The Science Club consists of a group of students who want to explore the many branches of engineering, mathematics, and science. The club is open to all students.

The purpose of the Science Club is to promote interest, understanding, and knowledge of the scientific world amongst college students and the local community. The Science Club is one of the largest clubs in the college. The club is divided into three sections: Physics, Chemistry, and General.

The club aims to arouse engineering students' interest in learning science, promoting academic achievement in Science subjects. It also helps students to learn more about science outside the classroom and teaches how to apply scientific knowledge in daily life through activities. The club also guides students to develop an independent, logical and creative mind.

Activities include experiments in: making electronic components, making invisible ink, electroplating, biodiesels, fire writing, and making venation of leaves. Information about the science club and day-to-day updates are displayed on the notice board.

Objectives of Science Club: The five Guiding Principles govern how activities have been developed. Investigator activity offers students the opportunity to:

1. Solve a relevant, science-based problem set within a scenario.
2. Work in pairs or small groups or independently.
3. Take part in practical, hands-on science activities.
4. Think and talk about science during the activity and when sharing results.
5. Share results using a variety of media.

Things to Do in Science Club:

MSAJCE Science Club

- Conduct Seminars, Workshops, Exhibitions and Quiz competitions to inculcate knowledge in the field of science.
- Make students acquire knowledge by giving lectures once a month.
- Promote the use of IT in learning science.
- Encourage student participation in activities organized by the Science Club.
- Help students promote discussion on recent innovations in the field of science once a month.
- Decorate the walls of the classroom, library and laboratory with scientific pictures and charts prepared by students themselves.

5. Coding Club — About, Aim and Practice

MSAJCE Coding Club

About: Programming abilities are crucial tools that can be applied to and integrated into a variety of disciplines and domains in today's climate of rapid change.

Aim:

1. More coding contests should be held to reach all students on campus.
2. Conduct workshops on computer science topics like web development, application development, machine learning, IoT, etc.
3. Participate in open source initiatives, create websites and applications, and offer technical assistance to clubs and groups on campus.
4. Represent the college at intercollegiate competitions.
5. Take part in competitions like ACM-ICPC, Google Code Jam, Google Summer of Code, and other online coding events.

Practice: The club holds a college-wide coding competition to find new members who are enthusiastic about coding. The competition emphasizes reasoning, analysis, and problem-solving abilities in addition to coding.

The club gets together once a week to talk and work on initiatives. To accomplish a shared goal, all members work together. Workshops and technical discussions on various competitions and computer science topics are conducted through the club.

Lecture sessions are also organized for the benefit of members, where some computer science principles are taught.

The group also hosts seminars and workshops by seniors and former members on how to prepare for internships and job placement.
